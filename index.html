❖ Common Name: Mangrove Tree

❖ Scientific Name: Rhizophora spp.

❖ Classification:

Kingdom: Plantae

Family: Rhizophoraceae

Order: Malpighiales

Soil Type:

  • Grows in saline, waterlogged, and often oxygen-poor soils found in coastal and estuarine areas. Mangroves thrive in muddy, sandy, or loamy substrates that can retain moisture.

Life Span:

  • Mangroves can live for 30 to 50 years, though some may survive longer under ideal conditions.

Pests and Diseases:

  • Pests: Leaf-eating insects and borers occasionally affect mangroves but are generally not a major issue due to their salt tolerance.
  • Diseases: Fungal infections such as leaf spot and canker are common, particularly in dense or stagnant environments.

Medical Uses:

  • Antibacterial and Antifungal: Mangrove extracts are used to treat bacterial and fungal infections.
  • Wound Healing: Leaves and bark have antiseptic properties and are used to promote wound healing.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Leaves and bark extracts are traditionally used for reducing swelling and treating skin inflammation.
  • Gastrointestinal Health: Bark decoctions have been used to relieve digestive issues, including diarrhea and dysentery.
